CISP 360: Introduction to Structured Programming

IMPORTANT: Please login to D2L prior to our first meeting on March 15, 2016 and complete the online orientation.

Prerequisite:CISP 300 with a grade of "C" or better.
Description: This course is an introduction to structured programming. The topics covered include: top-down design, input/output considerations, control structures and flow control, variables, constants, the use of libraries, simple to intermediate data structures, functions, and arguments. An introduction into objects will be included.

This is a hybrid course in which approximately half of the class is online. Students must have access to a computer and the Internet and have an active email address. The class will meet on campus during the times listed below. You may want to bring your own laptop to class. We will be working in the lab each week.
